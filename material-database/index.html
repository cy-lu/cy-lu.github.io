Mathematical model definitions

Rational-pole model

The rational-pole representation is written as

\[ \varepsilon_{\mathrm{RP}}(z)=\varepsilon_{\infty} +\sum_{j=1}^{N}\left[ \frac{r_j}{z-p_j}+\frac{r_j^{\ast}}{z-p_j^{\ast}} \right]. \]

Here \(z\) is the normalized complex-frequency variable, \(p_j\) is a complex pole, \(r_j\) is the corresponding complex residue, and \(N\) is the number of pole pairs. The conjugate-pair structure gives a real-valued time-domain response for real fields.

Critical-point model

The critical-point representation is written as

\[ \varepsilon_{\mathrm{CP}}(\omega)=\varepsilon_{\infty} +\sum_{j=1}^{N}\left[ \frac{c_j\Omega_j}{\Omega_j-\omega-i\Gamma_j} +\frac{c_j^{\ast}\Omega_j}{\Omega_j+\omega+i\Gamma_j} \right], \qquad \Omega_j>0,\;\Gamma_j>0. \]

Here \(\Omega_j\) is the transition frequency, \(\Gamma_j\) is the damping rate, \(c_j\) is a complex coefficient, and \(N\) is the number of critical-point terms. CP-family entries use this same analytic form.
